Lets again go back to the pulse rate data that we used previously. The data was supplied by Dr Richard J. Wilson, Department of Mathematics, University of Queensland. The following is the link for the data and the metadata: see here. Read the description and understand which variable means what.

When you try to practice at home, copy and run the following two lines first. This creates a dataframe called \(\texttt{pulse_data}\) that has the data from the above link.

pulse_data = read.table(url("http://www.statsci.org/data/oz/ms212.txt"), header = TRUE)
pulse_data = pulse_data[complete.cases(pulse_data), ]

D. Using the same two variables, can you demonstrate that the correlation between \(\mathbf{x}\) and \(\mathbf{y}\) is the same as the correlation between correlation between \(\mathbf{y}\) and \(\mathbf{x}\) \(\big(\)i.e., cor(\(\mathbf{y, x}\)) = cor(\(\mathbf{x, y}\)) \(\big)\)?

(Question 2): Regression Model


A. Build the above simple linear regression model in \(\texttt{R}\). Provide the estimated regression equation.

Answer: 

model = lm(Pulse2   ~ Pulse1, data = pulse_data )
model
## 
## Call:
## lm(formula = Pulse2 ~ Pulse1, data = pulse_data)
## 
## Coefficients:
## (Intercept)       Pulse1  
##     31.8476       0.8581

\[ \widehat{y} = 31.85 + 0.86 x\]


B. What is the interpretation of your estimated \(\widehat{\beta}_0\)?

Answer: Estimated Pulse2 when Pulse1 is 0.


C. What is the interpretation of your estimated \(\widehat{\beta}_1\)?

Answer: Estimated change in Pulse2 (y) when Pulse1 (x) changes by 1 unit.


D. Find and interpret \(R^2\), also denoted by the coefficient of determination.

Answer: 

summary(model)
## 
## Call:
## lm(formula = Pulse2 ~ Pulse1, data = pulse_data)
## 
## Residuals:
##    Min     1Q Median     3Q    Max 
## -29.09 -23.07 -17.92  28.08  72.65 
## 
## Coefficients:
##             Estimate Std. Error t value Pr(>|t|)    
## (Intercept)  31.8476    16.4415   1.937 0.055379 .  
## Pulse1        0.8581     0.2140   4.010 0.000113 ***
## ---
## Signif. codes:  0 '***' 0.001 '**' 0.01 '*' 0.05 '.' 0.1 ' ' 1
## 
## Residual standard error: 29.57 on 107 degrees of freedom
## Multiple R-squared:  0.1307, Adjusted R-squared:  0.1225 
## F-statistic: 16.08 on 1 and 107 DF,  p-value: 0.0001125

\(R^2 = 0.1307\).

\(13\%\) variability in y is explained by the model.


E. Verify that \(R^2\) is square of the corrleation coefficient between the two variables.

Answer: 

cor(Pulse1, Pulse2)^2
## [1] 0.1306681


F. Plot the regression line on top of the scatter plot of the data.

Answer: 

plot(Pulse1, Pulse2)
abline(model, col="red")
